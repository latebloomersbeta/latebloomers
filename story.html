How it happened

A rescue mission
since childhood

Where it started

Growing up next to a florist in Paris, Clem used to rescue the flowers left out for the bin each evening and bring them home to her mum, who had no idea where they were coming from until she figured it out. They weren't damaged or dead, just done for the day.

The idea

Years later, living in North London and still loving flowers but not always being able to afford them, the same thought kept coming back. Florists throw away perfectly good bouquets every single evening, and the people who'd love them never get the chance. The idea had been there for a long time. What was missing was the moment to act on it.

The push

Getting made redundant from the cultural industry was that moment. Around the same time, Clem met people from very different paths of life who had all done one thing in common: they'd started their own thing, and that was enough to go for it. Late Bloomers launched in London so that florists could list their end-of-day bunches and people nearby could pick them up before they went to waste.

What we're building

We're here for the independent florists who put care into what they make, and for the people who want flowers in their home without spending a fortune. It's a small platform, built slowly and honestly, starting in London, where nothing good goes in the bin if it doesn't have to.
